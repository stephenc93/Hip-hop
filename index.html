MCing

The Oral Pillar

  • Manifested from the social conditions of the time.
  • This form of poetic and “verbal acrobatics” was derived from ancient African culture and oral tradition.
  • Also known as “rapping” this element removed the veil that isolated the wider culture from the social conditions of many underserved urban communities.

    • DJing

    The Aural Pillar

  • This was a new found manipulation of sounds that was used to create music.
  • The innovative breaks and isolation of the percussive beat was what gave hip hop its initial rise.
  • This new form of DJing was started by Kool DJ Herc who created hip hop in the 1970’s.

    • Breakdance

    The Physical Pillar

  • Groups such as Shaka Zulu Kings, Zulu Queens and the Rock Steady Crew gave rise to B-Boying/B-Girling.
  • Breaking can be described as “poetry in motion”.
  • Its acrobatics style with influences of gymnastics, Capoeria, martial arts and other cultural influences speaks to the innovative wave ushered in by hip hop culture.

    • Graffiti

    The Visual Pillar

  • This is one of the most controversial of the elements.As most graffiti artist leave their artwork in public places and “tag” it by leaving their names.
  • TAKI 183, made this form of artistic representation famous and in neighborhood such as Wynwood, Little Haiti and Opa-locka we can see this art form’s widespread integration with bursts of energy and vibrancy on buildings throughout the cities.

    • Knowledge

    The Mental Pillar

  • This element is the thread that weaves all the other elements together.
  • “Knowledge of self” refers to the Afro-diasporic mix of spiritual and political consciousness designed to empower members of oppressed groups” according Travis Gosa in his book entitled The Fifth Element of Hip Hop: Knowledge.
